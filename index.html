Here, park yourself!

Everyone loves parks! Parks are like an oasis of urban life, where the bustle of cities meets the serenity of nature. From a city (Yangzhou) famous for its parks (specifically traditional Chinese gardens), I grew up appreciating the beauty of parks. Spring is the best time, of course—trees budding, birds chirping, water flowing, kids playing... And during my undergrad years in Beijing, I had the chance to explore so many incredible ones—too many to name!

Many of these traditional Chinese gardens were once imperial retreats or the private estates of historical figures. Today, they've been transformed into public parks, and are open to everyone (some may require a ticket). You can easily find quiet corners under lush trees, surrounded by grand landscapes steeped in history. Anyways, whether you are seeking a walk through of history, or simply a peaceful spot to idle away the day, these parks are here for you. This website takes you on a journey to a few parks in Beijing and Yangzhou, sharing some fun facts along the way. Enjoy!

Which park in Yangzhou also features a notable white pagoda, intentionly modeled after the one in Beihai Park?

*Slender West Lake*
Address: Yangzhou City, Jiangsu Province, China

Legend has it that the White Pagoda in the Lotus Tower in Yangzhou's Slender West Lake Scenic Area was inspired by Beihai Park's White Pagoda. During the Qianlong Emperor's famous southern tours—because what's an Emperor without a bit of flair, especially when it's Qianlong—the lake was dredged and expanded to fit his grand entourage. While admiring the view, Qianlong casually mentioned how it reminded him of Beihai Park, and it would be more fantastic to have the same White Pagoda here. Cue the local salt merchant, eager to win royal favour. Overnight, he had a pagoda built to mimic the one in Beihai Park. By morning, the magic happened, a pagoda mimicking Beihai's stood tall, and even the Emperor was impressed! The story is a classic flex of imperial power and the insane wealth of Yangzhou's salt merchants. Yet, while empires crumble, Slender West lake remains—a masterpiece of the blend of the nature beauty and ancient Chinese garden artistry. Today, the beauty is equal to everyone—just grab a ticket, and you can enjoy its charm like an emperor!
